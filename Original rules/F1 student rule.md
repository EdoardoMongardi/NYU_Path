# F-1 and J-1 International Student Rules

## Full-Time Enrollment Requirement

All F-1 and J-1 international students must register full-time every semester or receive permission from OGS for a **reduced course load (RCL)**, which is anything less than full-time registration.

- **Undergraduate students**: a minimum of **12 credits** is considered full-time enrollment.
- **Graduate students**: typically a minimum of **9 credits** is considered full-time enrollment. However, you should confirm with your academic department as to what full-time enrollment is for your academic program.

If you register or drop below full-time **without OGS permission AND the support of your academic department**, you will lose the benefits of F-1 and J-1 status and you will be unable to stay and study in the US, and this may impact your eligibility for F-1 CPT or OPT or J-1 Academic Training.

If you have concerns about course enrollment during your final semester, speak with an OGS advisor before your course registration is complete.

## Reduced Course Load Options

### Reduced Course Load in Your Final Semester

If it is your final semester before you complete your academic program and you have fewer than full-time credits remaining to finish your degree or program, learn more about the process to apply for a reduced course load in your final semester.

### Medical Reduced Course Load

If you have a medical condition requiring you to take a leave of absence or reduced course load, learn more about the process to apply for a medical reduced course load.

### Other Academic Reasons for a Reduced Course Load

If you have any other reason that prevents you from registering full-time, please speak to an OGS advisor during our advising hours. Other reasons may include initial difficulty with the English language or reading requirements, unfamiliarity with US teaching methods, or improper course level placement.

> **Special Note about Eligibility**
>
> Before withdrawing from courses and applying for a reduced course load, please contact your academic department to confirm your eligibility to continue in your program. OGS cannot authorize a medical reduced course load where you are registered for zero credits without confirmation that you are eligible to continue your studies after your leave.

## Maintaining F-1/J-1 Status

As an international student in the US, it's crucial for you to grasp the necessity of maintaining your F-1/J-1 status in compliance with US immigration laws throughout the duration of your studies in the US. Make sure you follow this information while you are an international student at NYU.

Not following these immigration requirements will lead to the **termination of your F-1 or J-1 status**, necessitating the process of regaining it, which can be both time-consuming and expensive.

Moreover, there may be further immigration repercussions in the future, including:

- Difficulty with or denial of future OPT requests
- Difficulty with or denial of future visa or change of status applications such as H1B, O1, Permanent Residency, etc.
- Difficulty with or denial of F-1 visa renewal, especially if attempting to renew it while outside the US during a final semester consisting solely of online classes.

## F-1 and J-1 Student Legal Requirements

### Keep Your Immigration Documents Valid and Unexpired

- **Passport**: should be valid at least six months into the future at all times. If your passport is expiring soon, renew it with your country's consulate in the US or in your home country.
- **I-94 record**: should be correct every time you reenter the US to ensure you entered the country in F-1 or J-1 status. Save a copy of your I-94 for your records after each entry. To learn more, visit I-94 Record.
- **I-20 or DS-2019**: must be accurate and unexpired. You must keep all original I-20s ever issued to you in a safe place. Do not throw away any I-20. If any information on your I-20 or DS-2019 is incorrect, please contact us. If your I-20 or DS-2019 is expiring, learn more about getting an extension.
- **Visa**: must be valid if you are traveling outside of and reentering the US. To learn more about getting a new visa, visit get a US visa.

### Register for Full-Time Enrollment Every Fall and Spring Semester

- You are required to enroll in a full time course of study each Fall and Spring semester, unless you are authorized by OGS to have a reduced course load.
- **Undergraduate students** must enroll in at least **12 credits**.
- **Graduate students** usually must enroll in at least **9 credits** but can check with their academic department to ensure they are registered full time.
- If you are working on a thesis, dissertation, or final project, check with your department to see if you are eligible for full-time equivalency. The academic department must reflect your full-time equivalency in Albert before the add/drop deadline.
- You must be making normal progress towards your program completion.
- Full-time enrollment must be maintained throughout the semester. Withdrawn courses do not count towards full-time enrollment. If you think you need to enroll part-time, get OGS permission to register for a reduced course load before your registration is finalized.
- Continuing students are not required to enroll during the summer session.

### Limit Your Online Course Enrollment Per Required Semester (Fall/Spring)

You must register for in-person courses each Fall and Spring semester. Each course is assigned an instruction mode; 'in-person,' 'hybrid,' or 'blended' instruction modes are in-person courses.

**For F-1 students:**
- Only **one online course or 3 online credits** may count towards full-time enrollment each required semester (Fall/Spring) including your final semester.
- You may register less than full-time in your last semester with OGS reduced course load permission, but OGS still advises you to enroll in at least one in-person course. Additionally, students must remain in the US for their last semester if they intend to apply for Post-completion OPT.

**For J-1 students:**
- Only **one online course** counts towards full-time enrollment each required semester (Fall/Spring) including your final semester.
- You may register less than full-time in your last semester with OGS reduced course load permission, but you're required to enroll in at least one in-person course.

Once the full-time enrollment requirement is met — including online course enrollment minimums — additional courses are permitted whether in-person, hybrid or online.
